I have just returned from [ECIR 2026](https://ecir2026.eu), held in Delft, The Netherlands, from 29 March to 2 April 2026.

This year, I had the great pleasure of serving as a Short Paper Chair alongside [Sean MacAvaney](https://macavaney.us) and [Mohammad Aliannejadi](https://aliannejadi.com). The short paper track received 108 submissions, of which 37 were accepted (≈34% acceptance rate). I am really happy how soothly our team handled the process.

A fun editorial milestone: The conference proceedings were published in four parts---which means, we now have  4 DOIs as co-editors :-). (All short papers appear in Volume II, though.)

I also had the pleasure to serve as a mentor at the ECIR 2026 Doctoral Consortium, working with two excellent PhD students.

The main conference took place in [Lijm & Cultuur](https://www.lijmencultuur.nl)---a memorable and refreshingly unusual venue compared to the standard conference venues.
Delft was nice, too: while it welcomed me with very Dutch rain, it rewarded us with steadily improving weather over the days.
