Last week, [UMAP 2026](https://www.um.org/umap2026/) took place in Gothenburg, Sweden.

I am delighted that three of our papers were accepted for UMAP. It is a pity that I couldn't make it this time---so, I missed both the discussions and the opportunity to catch up with *academic friends*. I'm very grateful to my co-authors for presenting the work, and I am sure they did an excellent job!

## The papers
- [Anouk van Kasteren](https://anoukvankasteren.com/) led and presented our work, co-authored by [Marloes Vredenborg](https://marloesvredenborg.com/), [Judit Masthoff](https://www.uu.nl/staff/JFMMasthoff), and me. In this paper, we identified the decision-making strategies travellers employ when choosing which alternative route to take during public transport disruptions.

- [Judith Masthoff](https://www.uu.nl/staff/JFMMasthoff) presented our research led by [Marloes Vredenborg](https://marloesvredenborg.com/) and further co-authored by [Marit Bentvelzen](https://www.uu.nl/staff/MBentvelzen) and me. We show how situational context---particularly urgency---shapes user preferences for explanation tone.

- [Robin Verachtert](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=K1Md2qUAAAAJ&hl=en) presented our work on news recommender diversification, co-authored by [Kim Falk](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=C3eBvIYAAAAJ&hl=en) and me. The paper reports a large-scale comparison of three diversification methods deployed on [NU.nl](https://www.nu.nl) (a major Dutch online newspaper). It is particularly rewarding to see your research deployed in a large-scale production system and having immediate impact in the real world.
